Ratio of Children in 1880 NY

The choropleth map shows the % of children aged 16 and under for each county in New York state in 1880. Some areas in the northeast, south, and west had around 40% of children and these places were greatly impacted by the industrialization and urbanization at that time.

Number of Children in the U.S. in 1880

The graduated symbol map shows the number of children aged 16 and under in the U.S. in 1880. Children were primarily living in the eastern part of the country. California, which experienced the Gold Rush in the mid-19th century and attracted people, had a large number of children.

Yearly Total Precipitation in Iowa

The isarithmic mapping of yearly total precipitation in Iowa shows high precipitation in the northeast.

Ratio of Men & Women in Labor Force Age in 1880 NY

The bivariate choropleth map show the percentage of men in their labor force age (15-64) as well as that of women. In areas that were already urbanized or industrialized during this period, such as the southernmost areas that would later become New York City, the percentage of young men and women was high.

Historical Family Migration in the U.S.

This flow map shows the historical family migration in the U.S. bewtween 1887 and 1924. While Oklahoma and California saw large influxes of families during this period, many Midwestern and Southern states experienced a family drain.

Migration of Oswego Normal School Graduates into the States

Analysis on the geographical destribution of teachers who were trained at the Oswego Primary Teachers Training School, more commonly known as the Oswego Normal School, NY, in the late 19th tcentury.
